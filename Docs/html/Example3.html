Example 3: Calibrating the AR Drone 2.0

This example will show how the AR Drone 2.0 can be calibrated to compensate for any offsets in the accelerometer, gyroscope and magnetometer. The calibration is also used to find the scaling factors to normalize the accelerometer and magnetometer data before they can be used to calculate the drones position.

The calibration procedure is governed by a GUIDE application:

open('Example3_Calibrating_The_AR_Drone.fig')

After a wireless connection is achieved with the AR Drone 2.0 the calibration model can be uploaded onto the drone using the "Build and upload" button. When this is complete the desired sensor component to calibrate can be selected using the drop down menu. The calibration instructions panel will inform how the drone should be placed for the particular calibration.

Positioning of the drone:

Calibration of the accelerometers relys on the user positioning the drone in 6 different positions, to measure the accelertion imposed by earths gravitational field in all three axises in both positive and negative direction. It is imortant that the drone is stationairy during the calibration and that it is positioned such that only one axis senses the gravitational pull per calibration. Any offsets during this calibration will result in an offset in what the drone considers level.

The gyroscope can be calibrated by placing the drone on the ground and making sure it remains stationairy during the calibration

Calibration of the magnetometer requires the user to rotate the drone during the calibration measurement such that all three components of the magnetometer experience the maximum and minimum possible magnetism.
